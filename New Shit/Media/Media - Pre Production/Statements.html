I am going to create a video essay about the videogame Dark Souls 3 and its lore. It will be mainly educational with some opinions and comedy aspects as well. I feel that Dark Souls has a style of storytelling that is better than other games and I hope to change people’s opinion without disregarding or insulting a game that they love as inferior. It will be focused on how Dark Souls tells it its lore and how, in my opinion, its style is in most ways better than how other games tell their narrative. I will first show how Dark Souls tells its story then compare how Dark Souls tells its story in comparison to games like Call of Duty or The Last of Us. My contention will be that games that tell its story in a manner such as Dark Souls are better than forcing game play and narrative as it gives the player more freedom and satisfaction figuring it out. I decided on using a video essay as it is the easiest way to reach my audience who already watch videos. Throughout my essay, as I aim towards teens to young adults, I will include comedic moments and gameplay too keep their attention and to make viewing enjoyable rather than just a monotone information dump. I hope that people who watch my video will have a greater understanding of the world of Dark Souls 3 and its story, and I also hope that they begin to look at how the story is told in other games compared to Dark Souls. I don’t expect or aim for this video to be much more than a passion project and a love letter to the series. This is because most of the information regarding the lore and the theories surrounding the lore, I have gathered about it has been already gathered by other people who I will refer too. I don’t want to take credit for all the hard work they’ve put in as that is unfair to them.

The aim that I want to achieve with my narrative is to change people’s perspective on how narratives are told in video games by showing how Dark Soul’s style of story telling is better than most conventional story telling in video games. To start I will give a general introduction to story telling by comparing Movies, Books and Video Games and how they have their own ways of telling a narrative. I will then list some examples of games that have really good narratives but have flaws in their forced gameplay which leads to frustration. I’ll then clarify by point out that while these games are good at telling stories, they suffer as they force the player to play the games story instead of the story the player may want to play. To give an example of a game that doesn’t use this style of forced gameplay I’ll look at the Dark Souls franchise. I’ll point out the parts that do have some force gameplay but point out that it’s beneficial to its story instead of detrimental. I’ll also highlight the fact that the narrative isn’t even important to the actual playthrough. As the narrative can be confusing and people may think a game with no narrative is better than one with a narrative, I’ll outline the general story and an example of a side plot so the audience can get a general outline of the story. After I have finished showing how Dark Souls tells its story, I’ll contrast that with Skyrim and Metal Gear. I’m specifically using these series as Metal Gear is notorious for having absurdly long cutscenes(it hold the record for longest cutscenes in a game and longest single cut scene) and Skyrim because it’s dialog is often inorganic and mechanical which can detract and it forces the player to sit through all of it without being able to do much. To finish I’ll state my contention that video games are better off having a hands-off approach than forcing the player to make certain actions.
