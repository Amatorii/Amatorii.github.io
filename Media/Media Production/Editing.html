Interviews:

   - The general purpose of this scene was to show that you could play Dark Souls and not know that it had a story. To do this I asked one of my friends who had played the game to show that even though he had played it (a fact which the audience knows is implied, therefore removing the necessity of justification) he didn’t know what the story was. I also showed an interview with my mum as a comedic touch. While my mum has played the Dark Souls series, the implied humour is that ‘Of course your mum doesn’t know the story of Dark Souls as she probably hasn’t every touched a video game with a ten-foot pole.’ This is evident from her general confused responses and makes it funnier as the audience think that she has no idea what I’m talking about and find her confusion funny as she’s presented to be out of her depth.

04:53 – 05:31:

   - This whole scene is also meant as satire. As I spiral from asking relevant questions to going on a rant about unrelated topics. As I get further and further from topic, the comedic tone is also signalled as things get more and more absurd. I go from showing in game photos to showing memes (which Dark Souls fans and my general audience will understand), to crude edits of serious characters made to look wacky. This culminates into me talking about a recent political event, Joe Biden playing a meme that is considered dead as he wants to seem relatable. I’m able to make the audience laugh by saying what it is they’re thinking as in Joe’s attempt to make himself appear like he knows pop culture, it’s illustrating further that he has no idea what he’s talking about. The final meme punctuates this as it’s a distortion meme. The eccentric nature of the meme and the absurd amounts of edits made to the photo is done to put a spotlight on how hard Joe is trying to seem funny to a teenage audience, whilst further cementing him as someone unaware that his actions have the inverse effect.

List of Editing Techniques (Note: Time Stamps are in referance to the video):

Standard Cut / Fade to Black - 00:04

   - This was used as just a simple transition. If I wanted to transition clips or topics without jarring the audience then I’d use this technique. If I just used plain cuts, it would be very unsatisfying for the audience

Jump Cut - 00:05

   - Used in a similar way to the Fade to Black but if I didn’t feel the transition needed to be smooth. These cuts were a lot more fast passed

Montage - 00:36

   - A general staple of these sorts of video essays. They include a montage of clips that have reference to the topic being talked about or the clips shown are being directly referenced by the narrator. Both examples are used as they give the audience a visual aid and clarification, so they know for sure they’re on the same page as the writer.

J Cut - 01:38

   - Done to try and ease the audience from the quick showcase back to me talking. I chose this technique in the same vein as the Fade as I didn’t want to jar the audience

Continuity Error - 01:52

   - Bit of a stretch as it’s not the conventional continuity error. I did this as I wanted to play the clip again in slow motion with subtitles saying, “Are you good bro?” for comedic effect.

Slow Motion - 01:54
